Investment Goals

Emergency Goals (Next 3 Months)

These are immediate goals that require liquid funds for unforeseen circumstances, such as:

  • Job loss
  • Sudden sickness
  • Essential purchases (e.g., repairs, urgent replacements)

Recommended Investment Options:

  • Savings Account: A traditional savings account with instant access to funds is perfect for immediate liquidity.
  • Overnight Funds: Low-risk mutual funds that invest in overnight instruments with very short maturity, offering better returns than savings accounts with minimal risk.
  • Liquid Funds: Mutual funds investing in short-term debt instruments (with a maturity of up to 91 days) providing liquidity and slightly higher returns compared to savings accounts.

Ultra-Short-Term Goals (4-12 Months)

These are goals that fall in the near future, such as purchasing items or covering short-term expenses. Examples:

  • Buying an iPhone
  • Paying school fees

Recommended Investment Options:

  • Ultra-Short-Term Debt Funds: Mutual funds investing in short-duration debt instruments, ideal for goals with a timeframe of 4-12 months. They offer a balance between liquidity and slightly higher returns than savings accounts.

Short-Term Goals (1-3 Years)

These goals are for a relatively medium timeframe, such as saving for a vacation or paying off home EMIs. Examples:

  • Vacation
  • Home EMIs

Recommended Investment Options:

  • Bank and PSU Debt Funds: Funds investing in bonds issued by public sector banks or government institutions, offering safety and better returns than savings accounts, suitable for goals with a 1-3 year horizon.
  • Short-Term Debt Funds: Mutual funds investing in debt instruments with slightly longer durations, balancing returns with liquidity.
