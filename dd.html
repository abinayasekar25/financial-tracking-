Breakdown of the 28/36 Rule

28%: The first part of the rule suggests that no more than 28% of your gross monthly income should be allocated for housing expenses (such as mortgage payments, property taxes, and insurance).

36%: The second part of the rule states that no more than 36% of your gross monthly income should be spent on total debt payments. This includes all debts, not just housing (e.g., car loans, credit card payments, student loans).
